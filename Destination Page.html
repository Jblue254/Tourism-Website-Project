Our Destinations


Maasai Mara Kenya


The Maasai Mara National Reserve, located in southwestern Kenya, is a world-renowned wildlife haven known for its spectacular landscapes and incredible biodiversity. It is part of the larger Mara-Serengeti ecosystem, which extends into Tanzania's Serengeti National Park.Learn More


Eiffel Tower


Built by Gustave Eiffel's company for the 1889 World's Fair. Standing at about 330 meters (1,083 feet), it was the world's tallest man-made structure for 41 years. Initially intended to be temporary, its utility for scientific and radio communication purposes.It symbolizes Paris and French engineering prowess, known for its intricate design, regular repainting, and daily illuminations .Learn More


Statue of Liberty


When the Statue of Liberty was first unveiled in 1886, she was actually a shiny, reddish-brown, just like a brand-new copper penny. Over the next few decades, exposure to the elements (rain, wind, and air) caused the copper to oxidize, forming a protective layer called a "patina." This natural chemical reaction is what slowly transformed her into the iconic greenish-blue hue we see today.Learn More


Taj Mahal


This was a brilliant architectural choice made by its builders. In the unlikely event of an earthquake, these minarets are designed to fall away from the central tomb, protecting the main structure and the resting place of Mumtaz Mahal and Shah Jahan. It's a subtle lean that's hard to notice with the naked eye, but it's a testament to the incredible foresight and engineering genius of the 17th-century builders. Learn More
